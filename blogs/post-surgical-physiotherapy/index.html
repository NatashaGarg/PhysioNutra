The Critical Importance of Post-Operative Physiotherapy

After a surgical procedure, the body naturally protects the injured area, leading to stiffness, muscle weakness, and scar tissue formation. Without structured physiotherapy, you risk incomplete healing, chronic stiffness, and poor long-term functional outcomes. Post-surgical rehab is essential to guide the healing process correctly, preventing long-term complications.

Common Surgeries Requiring Physiotherapy Rehab:

  • Joint Replacements: Total Knee Replacement (TKR) and Total Hip Replacement (THR).
  • Ligament & Tendon Repair: ACL, PCL, Meniscus, Rotator Cuff, and Achilles tendon surgery.
  • Fracture Repair: Post-immobilization stiffness and strength loss.
  • Spinal Surgery: Discectomy or fusion procedures for back pain relief.

Our Multi-Phase Approach to Post-Surgical Recovery

At PhysioNutra Clinic, our rehabilitation programs are broken down into carefully managed phases, ensuring tissue safety while maximizing recovery gains. We work closely with your surgeon's protocol to deliver precise, tailored care.

Phase 1: Pain and Swelling Management

The immediate focus post-surgery is to control inflammation and manage pain. We use non-invasive modalities like advanced Laser Therapy, TENS, and gentle joint mobilization techniques to minimize reliance on strong painkillers and initiate early, safe movement.

Phase 2: Restoring Range of Motion and Flexibility

Stiffness is a primary threat to recovery. This phase introduces controlled passive and active range-of-motion exercises, crucial for joint health and preventing scar tissue from limiting future movement. Specific manual therapy techniques are used to gently increase mobility in the surgical joint.

Phase 3: Regaining Strength and Function

Muscle wasting occurs quickly after surgery. We implement progressive strengthening exercises, starting with gentle isometrics and progressing to resistance training. This stage is vital for rebuilding stability and the power required for daily activities, walking, and climbing stairs.

Phase 4: Return to Sport or High-Level Activity

The final stage focuses on advanced functional training, including agility drills, plyometrics, and sport-specific movements. Our goal is not just recovery, but a safe return to pre-injury levels of activity, ensuring long-term injury prevention.
