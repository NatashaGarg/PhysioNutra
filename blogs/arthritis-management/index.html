Arthritis Management Through Physiotherapy: A Complete Evidence-Based Guide

January 7, 2025
15 min read

Living with arthritis doesn't mean accepting a life of pain and limited mobility. With over 528 million people worldwide affected by arthritis, it's one of the most common causes of chronic pain and disability. However, evidence-based physiotherapy has emerged as a cornerstone of effective arthritis management.

üî¨ Evidence-Based Arthritis Care

Physiotherapy for arthritis is strongly supported by scientific research. A clinical review confirms that supervised exercise therapy provides at least as effective pain relief as pharmaceutical pain medications for knee and hip osteoarthritis.

Understanding Arthritis: Types and Symptoms

1. Osteoarthritis (OA) - "Wear and Tear" Arthritis

Osteoarthritis is the most common form of arthritis, affecting approximately 344 million individuals globally.

Common OA Symptoms:

  • Joint pain that worsens with activity
  • Morning stiffness lasting less than 30 minutes
  • Joint swelling and reduced range of motion

2. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) - Autoimmune Arthritis

Rheumatoid arthritis is a chronic systemic inflammatory disease.

Common RA Symptoms:

  • Joint pain, swelling, and warmth
  • Morning stiffness lasting more than 30 minutes
  • Fatigue and low-grade fever

Key Difference

Osteoarthritis is mechanical wear and tear affecting individual joints, while rheumatoid arthritis is an inflammatory autoimmune condition affecting multiple joints symmetrically.

Why Physiotherapy is Essential

Evidence-Based Benefits:

  1. Pain Reduction as Effective as Medication
  2. Improved Function and Mobility
  3. Reduced Joint Stiffness
  4. Stronger Supporting Muscles

Core Components of Physiotherapy

1. Supervised Exercise Therapy

Exercise is the cornerstone of arthritis management.

Types of Exercises We Prescribe:

  • Strengthening Exercises
  • Range of Motion Exercises
  • Aerobic Conditioning

2. Manual Therapy Techniques

  • Joint Mobilization
  • Soft Tissue Massage

Important Note

Passive interventions should play only a subordinate role to active exercise therapy.

Conclusion

Arthritis doesn't have to limit your life. With evidence-based physiotherapy, you can significantly reduce pain and improve function.
