What Causes Ulnar Nerve Impingement?

The **ulnar nerve** is vulnerable at the elbow as it passes through a narrow space called the **cubital tunnel**. Impingement often results from:

  1. **Prolonged Elbow Flexion:** Keeping the elbow bent for long periods (e.g., sleeping, holding a phone, desk work) stretches the nerve.
  2. **Direct Pressure:** Leaning on the elbow repeatedly (e.g., while driving or working at a desk).
  3. **Anatomical Issues:** The nerve slipping out of place when the elbow bends, or bone spurs constricting the tunnel.

Specialized Physiotherapy Treatment in Zirakpur

Our goal is to decompress the nerve, restore its ability to glide freely, and strengthen the muscles it supplies. We focus on non-surgical, evidence-based therapies.

Key Physiotherapy Techniques for Ulnar Nerve Relief:

  • Nerve Gliding Exercises: Specific, gentle movements designed to "floss" the ulnar nerve through the cubital tunnel, reducing sensitivity and freeing it from surrounding tissues.
  • Manual Therapy: Targeted soft tissue release and joint mobilization around the elbow, shoulder, and neck to relieve tension that may be contributing to the compression.
  • Ergonomic and Postural Correction: Identifying and modifying daily habits (like elbow positioning during sleep or work) that contribute to the impingement.
  • Strengthening & Endurance: Progressive exercises to improve the strength of the intrinsic hand muscles (those responsible for fine motor skills) and the muscles stabilizing the shoulder and forearm.
