Understanding Plantar Fasciitis: Causes of Your Chronic Heel Pain

Plantar Fasciitis involves inflammation of the plantar fascia—the thick band of tissue that runs across the bottom of your foot and connects your heel bone to your toes. This condition is often caused by factors like tight calf muscles, unsupportive footwear, sudden increase in activity, or poor foot mechanics.

Common Symptoms of Plantar Fasciitis:

  • Sharp, stabbing pain in the heel, especially with the first steps in the morning.
  • Pain that subsides during the day but worsens after long periods of standing or getting up from sitting.
  • Tenderness along the bottom of the foot near the heel.
  • Stiffness and difficulty flexing the foot.

The Role of Physiotherapy in Plantar Fasciitis Treatment

Unlike painkillers that only mask the symptoms, physiotherapy provides a comprehensive plan to heal the tissue, strengthen supporting muscles, and correct biomechanical faults. Our approach focuses on restoring flexibility and strength, ensuring a durable recovery.

Targeted Physiotherapy Exercises for Plantar Fasciitis

A crucial part of your recovery is a personalized exercise program. These exercises are designed to stretch and strengthen the structures that support your arch and heel, preventing future flare-ups. Consistency is key for lasting relief from foot pain.

Essential Exercises for Foot and Heel Recovery:

  1. Plantar Fascia Stretch: Rolling the arch of the foot over a frozen water bottle or tennis ball.
  2. Calf Stretches: Wall stretches targeting the gastrocnemius and soleus muscles.
  3. Towel Scrunches: Strengthening the small intrinsic muscles of the foot.
  4. Toe Spreads and Lifts: Improving stability and muscle control in the arch.
