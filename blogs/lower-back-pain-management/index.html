Understanding the Root Causes of Your Lower Back Pain

The complexity of the lumbar spine (lower back) means pain can stem from muscles, discs, joints, or nerves. Effective treatment begins with an accurate diagnosis. Our expert physiotherapists conduct a thorough assessment to pinpoint the exact source of your pain.

Common Lower Back Conditions We Successfully Treat:

  • Lumbar Disc Herniation (Slipped Disc): Where the inner material of a spinal disc pushes out, often causing Sciatica.
  • Sciatica: Pain that radiates from the lower back down the leg due to compression of the sciatic nerve.
  • Spinal Stenosis: Narrowing of the spinal canal, which puts pressure on the nerves.
  • Sacroiliac (SI) Joint Dysfunction: Pain caused by abnormal movement or alignment in the joint connecting the spine and pelvis.
  • Non-Specific LBP: Pain caused by muscle strain, ligament sprain, or poor posture.

The PhysioNutra Approach to Lower Back Pain Treatment

We use a combination of cutting-edge techniques and therapeutic exercises proven to be highly effective in managing and eliminating lower back pain. Our goal is not just temporary relief, but a complete functional restoration.

Key Physiotherapy Treatments for Back Pain:

  1. Core Stabilization Training: Activating and strengthening the deep abdominal and back muscles is crucial for supporting the spine and preventing future injury.
  2. Spinal Mobilization and Manipulation: Gentle hands-on techniques to improve joint movement, reduce stiffness, and restore natural spinal alignment.
  3. McKenzie Method (MDT): A proven protocol using specific directional movements and patient education to centralize and often eliminate disc-related pain and Sciatica.
  4. Advanced Modalities: Including Intermittent Lumbar Traction for disc decompression, TENS, and deep tissue release for muscle spasms.

Sciatica Relief: Targeted Nerve Decompression

Sciatica, characterized by pain, numbness, or weakness in the leg, requires targeted treatment. Our physiotherapy programs are specifically designed to reduce nerve root irritation. This includes gentle nerve glides, mobility exercises, and, where appropriate, mechanical traction to relieve pressure on the sciatic nerve.
