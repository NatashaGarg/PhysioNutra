**Physiotherapy (or physical therapy) is a healthcare profession that helps individuals restore, maintain, and maximize their physical strength, function, and movement.** You may need it if you are suffering from acute or chronic pain, recovering from a surgery or injury (sports or accident-related), or managing a chronic condition (like arthritis, stroke, or Parkinson's). Your first visit, the **Initial Assessment**, will involve a detailed discussion about your medical history, symptoms, lifestyle, and recovery goals. Your therapist will then perform a **physical examination** to assess your posture, range of motion, strength, and any painful movements. Based on this, a personalized treatment plan will be created. Please wear **comfortable, loose-fitting clothes**.

Each session at our clinic typically lasts between **45 to 60 minutes**. The total number of sessions needed varies greatly depending on the condition's severity, how long you've had it, and your body's response to treatment. After your initial assessment, your physiotherapist will discuss a recommended **treatment timeline** with you, focusing on the shortest path to lasting recovery.

While some manual techniques or therapeutic exercises may cause **temporary discomfort** as you work to restore movement, your physiotherapist will always operate within your **pain tolerance**.
