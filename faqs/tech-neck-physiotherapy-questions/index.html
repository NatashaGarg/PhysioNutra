Tech Neck Physiotherapy FAQs & Answers

Your guide to understanding and treating neck pain from device use in Zirakpur.

Home / Blogs / Tech Neck FAQs
What is Tech Neck and how is it diagnosed? +

Tech Neck is a modern repetitive strain injury where the head is held in a forward, downward position while looking at a mobile device or computer screen. This posture dramatically increases the strain on the cervical spine. Diagnosis at PhysioNutra Clinic involves a physical assessment of your posture, range of motion, muscle strength, and palpation to identify tight, painful trigger points in the neck and upper back.

Can Tech Neck cause headaches? +

Yes, absolutely. One of the most common associated symptoms is a cervicogenic headache. The excessive tension and tightness in the deep neck muscles (like the suboccipital and upper trapezius) irritate nerves that refer pain up into the head, often felt as a dull ache or tension band around the forehead or behind the eyes. Our treatment specifically targets these muscles to relieve the pain.

How long does it take for physiotherapy to fix Tech Neck? +

The duration of treatment varies based on the severity and how long you‚Äôve had the condition. Typically, significant pain relief can be achieved within 4-6 sessions. Full posture correction and long-term stability, which involves strengthening weakened muscles, usually takes 6-12 weeks with consistent in-clinic therapy and adherence to the at-home exercise program.

What physiotherapy treatments are used for Tech Neck? +

We use a comprehensive approach, which may include: Manual Therapy (joint mobilization and soft tissue release), Dry Needling to release stubborn trigger points, therapeutic exercises for deep neck flexor strengthening and postural correction, and advanced modalities like TENS/Ultrasound for acute pain relief.

Is Tech Neck a permanent condition? +

No, it is not permanent. Tech Neck is a postural dysfunction and muscle imbalance that is highly treatable. With consistent physiotherapy, corrective exercises, and conscious changes to your device usage and work ergonomics, you can fully reverse the condition and prevent its recurrence. Consistency is key!

Do you offer ergonomic advice for preventing Tech Neck? +

Absolutely. A key part of our Tech Neck therapy is personalized ergonomic consultation. We provide specific recommendations on monitor height, chair posture, and practical tips like the '20-20-20 rule' (looking away from your screen every 20 minutes for 20 seconds at something 20 feet away) and the correct way to hold your phone to minimize strain and prevent future pain.
