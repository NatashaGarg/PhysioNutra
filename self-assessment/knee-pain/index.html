Knee Pain FAQs

Answers to common questions about knee pain, diagnosis, and physiotherapy treatment in Chandigarh Tricity

The most common causes of knee pain include knee osteoarthritis (cartilage wear-and-tear), ACL or ligament tears (from sudden twisting), meniscus tears (cartilage damage inside the joint), patellofemoral pain syndrome (runner's knee), IT band syndrome, patellar tendinitis, and knee bursitis. The correct diagnosis requires a clinical assessment by a qualified physiotherapist.
You should see a physiotherapist if your knee pain has lasted more than two weeks, is affecting daily activities such as walking or climbing stairs, followed a sudden injury or twist, involves swelling, instability, locking, or clicking, or has not improved with rest. Early physiotherapy at PhysioNutra Clinic can prevent chronic problems and often avoids the need for surgery.
