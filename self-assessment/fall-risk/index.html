Fall History & General Health

Previous falls are the single strongest predictor of future falls. Answer on behalf of the elderly person if completing as a caregiver.

1Have there been any falls in the past 12 months?

3How many prescription medications does the person take daily?

Section 2 of 3

Balance, Strength & Mobility

Balance and lower limb weakness are the most modifiable fall risk factors — and the primary focus of physiotherapy.

5Can the person stand from a chair without using their arms to push up?
6How would you describe the person's walking?
7Does the person have any difficulty with balance — feeling unsteady or dizzy?
8Does the person have any fear of falling that limits their activity?
Section 3 of 3

Home Environment & Vision

Most falls happen at home. Environmental hazards are often easy to modify but frequently overlooked.

9Are there loose rugs, uneven floors, or significant clutter in the home walkways?
10Are grab rails fitted in the bathroom and toilet?
11Does the person have any problems with their vision?
12Does the person currently receive any physiotherapy or regular exercise for balance and strength?
