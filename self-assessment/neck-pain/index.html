Neck Pain FAQs

Evidence-based answers to common questions about tech neck and cervical pain

Tech neck is a repetitive stress injury caused by holding the head forward and downward while using smartphones or computers. For every inch the head moves forward, the effective weight on the cervical spine increases by approximately 10 lbs. Looking down at 60° places up to 60 lbs of force on the neck, straining muscles, discs, and joints over time. IT professionals and students in Chandigarh Tricity are particularly affected. Early physiotherapy intervention can fully reverse the condition.
Neck pain that radiates down the arm, causes numbness, tingling, or weakness in the hands, or is accompanied by difficulty gripping objects may indicate cervical radiculopathy or nerve compression. These symptoms require professional evaluation within 24–48 hours. PhysioNutra Clinic offers same-day appointments — call +91 94177 91833. Severe pain after trauma, or pain with fever or weight loss, should prompt immediate medical attention.
