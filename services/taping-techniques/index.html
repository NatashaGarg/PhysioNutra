What is Therapeutic Taping?

Therapeutic Taping: What You Need to Know Before Starting

Therapeutic taping uses specialised elastic or rigid tape applied to the skin to support muscles and joints, reduce pain, improve circulation, and enhance athletic performance. At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg and our certified physiotherapists are experts in kinesiology taping, sports taping, and McConnell taping.

Unlike traditional bandages or rigid braces, therapeutic tape works with your body’s natural movement patterns — providing targeted support without restricting motion. It can be used as a standalone treatment or as part of a comprehensive physiotherapy programme, and lasts 3–5 days even through showering and exercise.

Our Taping Techniques

Types of Therapeutic Taping We Offer

Different techniques for different needs — all applied by certified physiotherapists

Kinesiology Taping (KT Tape)

The most versatile elastic therapeutic tape that mimics skin elasticity. Applied with varying tension to lift the skin, improve lymphatic and blood flow, and provide gentle muscle support — lasting 3–5 days through activity.

  • Elastic, breathable, and water-resistant
  • Reduces pain, swelling, and inflammation
  • Supports muscles without restricting motion
  • Ideal for sports and active rehabilitation
  • Lasts 3–5 days with proper care

Sports Taping

Rigid or semi-rigid tape used for immediate joint stabilisation and injury prevention during sport. Provides maximum support for high-impact movements, limiting unwanted joint motion during competition.

  • Prevents ankle sprains and ligament injuries
  • Restricts dangerous joint ranges of motion
  • Essential for contact and pivoting sports
  • Supports ligaments during athletic events
  • Applied pre-competition or during rehab

McConnell Taping

Rigid taping designed for patellofemoral pain (kneecap pain) and postural correction. Provides immediate pain relief by biomechanically realigning the patella — often producing relief within a single session.

  • Corrects kneecap tracking and alignment
  • Immediate pain reduction in knee conditions
  • Used for shoulder and spinal posture correction
  • Aids quadriceps re-activation post-injury
  • Complementary to strength-based rehab

Your Session

The Taping Application Process

What to expect when you come in for a therapeutic taping session at PhysioNutra Clinic

1

Assessment & Evaluation

Our physiotherapist evaluates your condition, identifies problem areas, assesses movement patterns, and determines the most appropriate taping technique — kinesiology, sports, or McConnell — for your specific needs and activity level.

2

Skin Preparation

The area is cleaned and thoroughly dried. Body hair may be trimmed where necessary for better adhesion. Proper preparation is essential for optimal tape adherence and minimising skin irritation — especially for sensitive skin types.

3

Precise Tape Application

Tape is applied with specific tension, direction, and pattern based on your condition. The physiotherapist uses expert technique to ensure correct positioning and optimal therapeutic effect — direction and tension vary between facilitation, inhibition, and lymphatic applications.

4

Activation & Movement Testing

The tape is activated by friction (rubbing) to generate heat and maximise adhesion. You’ll be asked to perform relevant movements to confirm comfort, correct positioning, and that the tape provides the intended support or correction.

5

Aftercare Instructions

You receive full guidance on how to care for the tape: when to remove it, how to remove it safely, activities that are safe, and signs to watch for. Kinesiology tape can last 3–5 days with proper care — pat dry after showering, never rub.

6

Follow-Up & Re-Taping

We monitor your progress across sessions and adjust the taping strategy as your condition evolves. Taping is most effective when integrated with strengthening exercises and manual therapy — a comprehensive approach we deliver at every visit.

Therapeutic Taping — Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about taping services in Chandigarh Tricity

Kinesiology taping uses elastic therapeutic tape applied to the skin to support muscles and joints without restricting movement. It works by lifting the skin slightly, decompressing pain receptors beneath, improving blood flow and lymphatic drainage, and enhancing proprioceptive (body position) feedback. The tape’s elasticity closely mimics human skin, allowing full movement while providing targeted support.
Quality kinesiology tape lasts 3–5 days, even through showers, swimming, and exercise. The tape is water-resistant and maintains its elasticity throughout. Pat the tape dry after getting wet rather than rubbing vigorously. Avoid applying oils or lotions to the taped area. Sports tape is typically removed after each event, while McConnell tape may need daily reapplication.
Therapeutic taping effectively treats sports injuries, ankle sprains, knee pain, shoulder impingement, tennis elbow, plantar fasciitis, lower back pain, neck pain, post-surgical swelling, lymphoedema, IT band syndrome, Achilles tendinopathy, hamstring strains, postural issues, and carpal tunnel syndrome. It can be used both preventatively and during rehabilitation.
Absolutely. Taping is widely used in professional and amateur sport for injury prevention, performance enhancement, and faster recovery. It supports joints and muscles without limiting range of motion — making it ideal for competition and training. It’s safe for all sports including football, basketball, cricket, running, swimming, and gym training.
Some simple applications can be self-applied after proper training. However, effective taping requires expertise in tape tension, direction, pattern, and positioning — all of which vary by condition. Incorrect application reduces benefit and can worsen some conditions. We recommend professional application and can teach you basic maintenance techniques for home use between sessions.
Taping is generally painless and comfortable. Some individuals with sensitive skin or adhesive allergies may experience mild irritation. We offer hypoallergenic tape options for sensitive skin. To minimise irritation: avoid applying tape over broken skin, remove it gently by pulling the tape back along itself (not lifting), and allow your skin to breathe between re-tapings.
