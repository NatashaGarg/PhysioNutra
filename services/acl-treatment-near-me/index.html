Our ACL Treatment Process

Step-by-step approach to your complete ACL recovery

1

Initial Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation including surgical details review, current status assessment, ROM and strength testing, functional limitations identification, and goal setting discussion. We create your personalized roadmap to recovery.

2

Phase 1: Early Recovery (0-6 weeks)

Focus on controlling pain and swelling, protecting the healing graft, achieving full knee extension and 120+ degrees flexion, quadriceps activation, normal gait restoration, and early strengthening exercises.

3

Phase 2: Strength Building (6-12 weeks)

Progressive resistance training, closed-chain exercises, proprioception and balance work, cardiovascular fitness maintenance, and continued ROM optimization. Building the foundation for advanced training.

4

Phase 3: Advanced Training (3-6 months)

High-intensity strength training, introduction of running and agility drills, plyometric exercises, sport-specific movements, power development, and progressive functional activities.

5

Phase 4: Return to Sport (6-9+ months)

Comprehensive functional testing, LSI >90% achievement on all measures, sport-specific training, psychological readiness assessment, gradual return-to-play protocol, and long-term injury prevention education.

6

Long-Term Maintenance

Continued strengthening program, injury prevention exercises, periodic check-ins and reassessment, ongoing support and guidance to minimize re-injury risk and optimize long-term knee health.

How long does ACL rehabilitation take?

+
Complete ACL rehabilitation typically takes 6-9 months for return to sport, with 9-12 months optimal for high-level athletes. Timeline varies based on surgery type, graft choice, associated injuries, and your individual progress. We use criteria-based progression, not arbitrary timelines, to ensure safe recovery. Rushing increases re-injury risk significantly.

Can I recover from ACL tear without surgery?

+
Some individuals with partial tears or those willing to avoid pivoting sports can manage without surgery through intensive "coper" rehabilitation. We provide comprehensive non-surgical ACL treatment including neuromuscular training, strengthening, bracing, and activity modification. However, complete tears in athletes wanting to return to pivoting sports typically require surgical reconstruction followed by rehabilitation.

When can I start ACL physiotherapy after surgery?

+
ACL rehabilitation begins immediately after surgery, often within 24-48 hours. Early physiotherapy is crucial for controlling swelling, preventing complications, achieving full knee extension, activating quadriceps, and starting gentle range of motion exercises. We coordinate closely with your surgeon to ensure safe, timely initiation of rehabilitation.

What is included in return-to-sport testing?

+
Our comprehensive return-to-sport testing includes isokinetic strength testing (quadriceps and hamstring), hop test battery (single hop, triple hop, crossover hop, timed hop), functional movement screening, agility and cutting assessment, sport-specific skills evaluation, and psychological readiness questionnaire. You must achieve >90% limb symmetry on all tests before clearance.
