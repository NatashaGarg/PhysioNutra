What is Neuro Rehab?

Neurological Rehabilitation: What You Need to Know Before Starting Treatment

Neurological rehabilitation is specialised physiotherapy designed to help individuals recover from conditions affecting the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nervous system. At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg leads a dedicated neuro rehab team using evidence-based interventions rooted in neuroplasticity science.

Whether you are recovering from a stroke, managing Parkinson’s disease or multiple sclerosis, or rehabilitating after a spinal cord injury, our comprehensive approach addresses your unique functional challenges. We focus on maximising independence, restoring movement, and helping you achieve personal goals through targeted therapeutic interventions.

Common Signs You Need Neuro Rehab

Symptoms that indicate neurological physiotherapy may help

  • Weakness or paralysis on one side of the body (hemiplegia)
  • Difficulty walking, poor balance, or frequent falls
  • Muscle stiffness, spasticity, or involuntary movements
  • Tremors, slow movement, or freezing of gait
  • Loss of coordination or fine motor control
  • Fatigue, numbness, or tingling linked to a neurological diagnosis

 Early neurological physiotherapy harnesses peak neuroplasticity for better outcomes. Do not wait — book an assessment with our neuro rehab team today.

Recovery Roadmap

Our Neurological Rehabilitation Process

A structured, phase-by-phase approach guided by functional milestones — not arbitrary timelines

1

Comprehensive Assessment (Day 1)

Thorough neurological evaluation: medical history review, standardised outcome measures (Barthel, Berg, MMSE), strength and tone testing, gait analysis, cognitive screening, and functional independence assessment. We create a personalised rehabilitation roadmap with clear milestone targets.

2

Acute Phase — Early Recovery (Weeks 0–4)

Goal: prevent secondary complications, initiate safe mobility, restore basic function. Focus on positioning, early mobilisation, preventing contractures and pressure injuries, basic transfer training, respiratory care where needed, and carer education. Home visits available throughout this phase.

3

Sub-Acute Phase — Active Rehabilitation (Weeks 4–12)

Progressive strengthening, balance and coordination training, gait retraining with or without aids, task-specific upper limb practice, spasticity management, and cognitive-motor dual-task exercises. Functional goals reviewed and updated at 4 weeks and 8 weeks.

4

Community Integration Phase (Months 3–6)

Outdoor mobility, stair training, return to driving assessment (where applicable), community activities, sport or recreation reintegration, and advanced functional tasks. Psychological wellbeing and adjustment to diagnosis addressed alongside physical goals.

5

Maintenance Programme (Ongoing)

For chronic neurological conditions (Parkinson’s, MS), a structured long-term maintenance programme prevents deconditioning, maintains achieved gains, and adapts as the condition progresses. Open access for any queries or sudden changes. Our goal is your long-term quality of life.

Targeted, repetitive, task-specific physiotherapy exercises stimulate this process — encouraging the brain to create alternative pathways around damaged areas. This is the scientific foundation of all modern neurological rehabilitation. The brain retains this capacity at any age, which is why it is never too late to begin therapy.
