What is Orthopedic Rehab?

Orthopedic Rehabilitation: What You Need to Know

Orthopedic rehabilitation is specialised physiotherapy focused on restoring function to bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, and tendons. Whether you are recovering from a fracture, joint replacement surgery, ligament tear, or managing a chronic condition like arthritis, structured rehabilitation is the cornerstone of a safe and complete recovery.

At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg leads every patient through a personalised, criteria-based programme rooted in the latest international evidence — restoring your mobility, strength, and quality of life faster and more safely.

When You Need Orthopedic Rehab

Signs you may need orthopedic rehabilitation

  • Pain, swelling, or stiffness in a joint after injury or surgery
  • Difficulty walking, climbing stairs, or performing daily activities
  • Reduced range of motion in the knee, hip, shoulder, or spine
  • Muscle weakness or imbalance after a period of immobility
  • Chronic joint pain from arthritis or degenerative conditions
  • Post-surgical recovery after fracture fixation or joint replacement

 Starting physiotherapy within 24–48 hours of surgery dramatically improves outcomes. Book a free assessment with us today — no referral needed.

Recovery Roadmap

Our Orthopedic Rehabilitation Process

A structured, phase-by-phase approach guided by objective milestones

1

Comprehensive Assessment (Day 1)

Thorough evaluation of your condition: medical history review, imaging interpretation, ROM and strength testing, swelling measurement, functional limitations, and goal-setting. We create a personalised roadmap with clear milestone targets for each phase.

2

Phase 1 — Pain Control & Protection (Weeks 1–4)

Goal: reduce pain and inflammation, protect healing tissues. Gentle ROM exercises, ice/heat therapy, electrical stimulation (TENS/EMS), laser therapy, and early isometric strengthening. Home physiotherapy available for this phase when mobility is restricted.

3

Phase 2 — Mobility & Early Strength (Weeks 4–8)

Progressive closed-chain exercises, manual therapy to address joint restrictions and soft tissue tightness, proprioception training, and continued ROM optimisation. Target: restore full range of motion and 60% baseline strength.

4

Phase 3 — Strength & Functional Training (Weeks 8–16)

Progressive resistance training, balance and coordination work, sport-specific or activity-specific movement patterns, cardiovascular conditioning, and endurance building. Objective strength testing determines readiness to advance.

5

Phase 4 — Return to Activity (Month 4 Onwards)

Full functional testing battery, activity-specific drills, sports return protocol where applicable, and criteria-based clearance. For joint replacements — return to driving, work, recreation, and sport with confidence.

6

Long-Term Maintenance (Ongoing)

Personalised home exercise programme, injury prevention education, periodic reassessment, and open access for any queries or concerns. Our goal is your long-term musculoskeletal health — not just a single episode of care.
