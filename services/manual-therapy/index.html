What is Manual Therapy?

Hands-On Treatment That Addresses the Root Cause of Your Pain

Manual therapy is a specialist form of physiotherapy delivered entirely through the hands — no machines, no drugs. At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg uses precisely applied pressure, controlled joint movements, and targeted soft tissue techniques to reduce pain, restore range of motion, and improve function.

It is suitable for both acute injuries and long-standing chronic pain. Every session is one-on-one, personalised to your exact condition, and grounded in the best available clinical evidence from international physiotherapy guidelines.

Our Techniques

Manual Therapy Techniques We Use

Every technique is selected and applied based on your individual assessment findings — never a one-size-fits-all approach

Joint Mobilisation

Gentle, controlled passive movements applied to a joint to restore normal glide, reduce stiffness, and relieve pain. Used for arthritis, frozen shoulder, ankle and wrist restrictions, and post-surgical stiffness.

  • Graded oscillatory techniques (Maitland Grades I–IV)
  • Sustained natural apophyseal glides (SNAGs)
  • Accessory and physiological joint movement
  • Peripheral and spinal joint mobilisation

Soft Tissue Massage

Targeted manual pressure applied to muscles, tendons, and ligaments to reduce pain, increase circulation, and promote tissue healing. Effective for muscle strains, chronic tension, and post-exercise soreness.

  • Deep tissue massage and cross-fibre frictions
  • Effleurage and petrissage techniques
  • Scar tissue mobilisation
  • Lymphatic drainage where indicated

Myofascial Release

Sustained manual pressure and skin-rolling techniques targeting the fascial network — the connective tissue web that surrounds muscles. Releases restrictions causing chronic pain, postural dysfunction, and limited movement.

  • Direct and indirect fascial techniques
  • Sustained release holds (up to 2–5 minutes)
  • Skin and subcutaneous tissue rolling
  • Functional release in movement

Spinal Manipulation

High-velocity, low-amplitude thrust applied to spinal joints to restore motion and reduce pain. Highly effective for mechanical back and neck pain. Always preceded by thorough assessment to ensure safety.

  • Lumbar, thoracic, and cervical manipulation
  • Sacroiliac joint manipulation
  • Mulligan SNAG and MWM techniques
  • Safety screening before every session

Trigger Point Therapy

Focused, sustained compression applied to hyperirritable muscle knots (trigger points) that cause local and referred pain patterns. Effective for tension headaches, shoulder pain, and myofascial pain syndrome.

  • Ischaemic compression and pressure release
  • Spray-and-stretch techniques
  • Integrated dry needling combination
  • Referred pain pattern mapping

Stretching & Neural Mobilisation

Passive and active stretching combined with neurodynamic techniques to restore nerve mobility, lengthen shortened tissues, and improve overall movement quality. Particularly effective for sciatica and radiculopathy.

  • Passive muscle and joint stretching
  • Proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation (PNF)
  • Straight leg raise and slump neurodynamics
  • Upper limb tension tests and mobilisation

Your Treatment Journey

What to Expect at PhysioNutra Clinic

A clear, structured pathway from your first visit to full recovery

1

Comprehensive Initial Assessment

A thorough 45–60 minute evaluation covering your medical history, posture, range of motion, joint play, muscle length, and neurological function. We identify the exact tissue at fault and the mechanical reason for your symptoms before any treatment begins.

2

Personalised Treatment Plan

We explain exactly which manual therapy techniques will be used and why, what the expected outcomes are, how many sessions we anticipate, and what your home exercise programme will involve. No surprises — full transparency from day one.

3

Hands-On Manual Therapy Sessions

Each treatment session lasts 30–45 minutes of focused, hands-on care. Techniques are applied precisely to the identified structures. We check your response during the session and adjust pressure, grade, and direction in real time based on your feedback and objective re-assessment.

4

Home Exercise Programme

Between sessions, you will receive specific exercises and self-mobilisation techniques that reinforce the in-clinic treatment. These are carefully chosen, demonstrated in clinic, and updated as you progress — ensuring continuous improvement between visits.

5

Progress Review & Discharge Planning

We formally re-assess your range of motion, pain, and function at key milestones. When treatment goals are met, we create a maintenance plan and teach you self-management strategies to prevent recurrence and maintain the gains you have worked hard to achieve.

Manual Therapy — Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about manual therapy in Chandigarh Tricity

Manual therapy is a hands-on physiotherapy approach in which a trained therapist uses their hands to mobilise joints, manipulate the spine, massage soft tissues, release myofascial restrictions, and address trigger points. At PhysioNutra Clinic, every manual therapy technique is applied based on your clinical assessment findings — not a generic protocol. The goal is to reduce pain, restore range of motion, and improve physical function without drugs or surgery.
PhysioNutra Clinic offers expert manual therapy from our main facility in Zirakpur (15 min from Chandigarh, 10 min from Mohali). We also have a branch in Sector 20, Panchkula and provide home-based manual therapy across the entire Tricity region including Kharar, Baltana, Dhakoli, and Peer Muchalla. Manual therapy should not be painful. Techniques such as joint mobilisation and soft tissue massage are applied gradually and within your comfort level. Some techniques — such as deep tissue massage or trigger point pressure — may produce a mild “good pain” sensation that most patients find relieving. You may experience mild muscle soreness for 24–48 hours after your first 1–2 sessions, similar to post-exercise soreness. Our therapists continually check your response and adjust accordingly.
Most patients with acute conditions see significant improvement within 3–6 sessions. Subacute and chronic conditions typically require 8–12 sessions. Complex or long-standing presentations may benefit from a 20-session programme. Your physiotherapist will give you a realistic estimate at your initial assessment and update the plan based on your rate of progress.
Yes. We provide home-based manual therapy sessions across Chandigarh, Mohali, Panchkula, Zirakpur, Kharar, and surrounding areas. Our therapists bring necessary equipment and deliver the same standard of care as our clinic. Home visits are particularly suited to elderly patients, post-surgical cases, and anyone with restricted mobility.
Regular massage primarily targets relaxation and general muscle tension. Manual therapy is a clinical, assessment-driven intervention performed by a qualified physiotherapist. It includes a full clinical diagnosis, specific joint mobilisation and manipulation techniques, neural mobilisation, myofascial release, and exercise prescription — all aimed at resolving the underlying musculoskeletal problem rather than providing temporary symptomatic relief.
Manual therapy is very safe when performed by a qualified, experienced physiotherapist. At PhysioNutra Clinic, all patients undergo thorough screening before any high-velocity technique. Spinal manipulation has a small risk of temporary local soreness; serious adverse events are extremely rare with properly screened patients. We always explain the risks and obtain consent before treatment.
