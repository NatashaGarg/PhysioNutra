What is Manual Therapy?

Manual therapy is a specialized form of physical therapy delivered with the hands rather than devices or machines. At PhysioNutra Clinic, our skilled physiotherapists use their hands to apply precise pressure, mobilize joints, and manipulate soft tissues to reduce pain, increase range of motion, and restore function.

This hands-on approach is highly effective for treating a wide range of musculoskeletal conditions, from acute injuries to chronic pain. Our therapists are trained in advanced manual therapy techniques and customize each treatment to your specific needs and goals.

Why Choose Manual Therapy?

  • Non-invasive and drug-free pain relief
  • Immediate improvement in mobility and function
  • Addresses root causes, not just symptoms
  • Personalized, one-on-one treatment
  • Complements other physiotherapy treatments
  • Evidence-based techniques with proven results

Our Manual Therapy Techniques

Comprehensive hands-on treatments tailored to your condition

Joint Mobilization

Gentle, controlled movements applied to joints to improve range of motion, reduce stiffness, and restore normal joint mechanics. Effective for arthritis, frozen shoulder, and post-injury recovery.

Soft Tissue Massage

Therapeutic massage techniques targeting muscles, tendons, and ligaments to reduce tension, improve circulation, and promote healing. Ideal for muscle strains and chronic pain.

Myofascial Release

Specialized technique that releases tension in the fascia (connective tissue) surrounding muscles. Helps with chronic pain, muscle tightness, and movement restrictions.

Spinal Manipulation

Precise, controlled force applied to spinal joints to improve alignment, reduce pain, and restore mobility. Highly effective for back and neck pain.

Stretching & Mobilization

Passive and active stretching techniques to improve flexibility, elongate tight muscles, and enhance overall movement patterns.

Trigger Point Therapy

Focused pressure applied to specific muscle knots (trigger points) to release tension, reduce referred pain, and restore normal muscle function.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about manual therapy

What is manual therapy?

+
Manual therapy is a hands-on treatment approach where skilled physiotherapists use their hands to manipulate, mobilize, and massage body tissues. This includes techniques like joint mobilization, soft tissue massage, myofascial release, and spinal manipulation to reduce pain, increase range of motion, and improve function.

Is manual therapy painful?

+
Manual therapy should not be painful. You may experience some discomfort or pressure during treatment, but it should be within your comfort level. Our therapists work within your pain tolerance and adjust techniques accordingly. Many patients find manual therapy relaxing and experience immediate relief.

How many manual therapy sessions will I need?

+
The number of sessions varies depending on your condition, severity, and response to treatment. Most patients see improvement within 3-6 sessions, but chronic conditions may require longer treatment. Your physiotherapist will create a personalized treatment plan and adjust it based on your progress.

How long does each manual therapy session last?

+
Each manual therapy session typically lasts 30-45 minutes. This includes hands-on treatment and may also incorporate exercises, stretches, or other complementary therapies. The initial assessment may take longer (60-90 minutes) to thoroughly evaluate your condition.

Can manual therapy help with chronic pain?

+
Yes! Manual therapy is highly effective for chronic pain conditions like lower back pain, neck pain, arthritis, and fibromyalgia. It addresses muscle tension, joint restrictions, and movement patterns that contribute to chronic pain. Combined with exercise and lifestyle modifications, it can provide significant long-term relief.

Is manual therapy safe?

+
Yes, manual therapy is very safe when performed by qualified, experienced physiotherapists. At PhysioNutra Clinic, our therapists are trained in advanced manual therapy techniques and conduct thorough assessments to ensure treatment is appropriate for your condition. Serious complications are extremely rare.

What should I wear to a manual therapy session?

+
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated. For example, shorts for leg/knee treatment, or a tank top for shoulder/back treatment. You may be asked to change into a gown for certain treatments to ensure proper access.

Will I feel sore after manual therapy?

+
Some mild soreness or stiffness for 24-48 hours after treatment is normal, similar to post-exercise soreness. This is a sign that your body is responding to treatment. Applying ice and staying hydrated can help. Most patients feel better within a day or two and notice improvement in their condition.
