What is Cupping Therapy?

Cupping therapy is an ancient healing practice used for thousands of years across various cultures. At PhysioNutra Clinic, we use specialized cups placed on the skin to create suction, drawing blood to the surface and promoting the body's natural healing processes. This time-tested technique effectively treats pain, inflammation, blood flow issues, and promotes overall wellness.

The suction created by cupping mobilizes blood flow to promote healing of injured tissues, releases fascial restrictions, reduces muscle tension, and stimulates the body's natural detoxification processes. Athletes, celebrities, and health-conscious individuals worldwide rely on cupping therapy for recovery and pain management.

Your Cupping Therapy Journey

What to expect during your session at PhysioNutra Clinic

1

Initial Consultation

Our physiotherapist assesses your condition, discusses your health goals, reviews medical history, and determines if cupping therapy is appropriate. We identify target areas and explain the procedure thoroughly.

2

Preparation

You'll be positioned comfortably, and the treatment area is prepared. We may apply oil to the skin for moving cupping techniques. All equipment is sterile and sanitized.

3

Cup Placement

Cups are applied to specific points using vacuum suction or fire method. You'll feel a tight pulling sensation as the cups draw blood to the surface, but it shouldn't be painful.

4

Treatment Duration

Cups remain in place for 5-15 minutes depending on the technique and condition. For moving cupping, cups glide across the treatment area throughout the session.

5

Cup Removal & Care

Cups are gently removed, leaving circular marks that typically fade within 3-7 days. We may apply soothing lotions and provide aftercare instructions for optimal results.

6

Follow-Up Plan

We develop a personalized treatment schedule based on your condition. Most patients see results within 2-4 sessions, scheduled 3-7 days apart for optimal healing.

Safety & Important Information

Understanding cupping marks and ensuring safe treatment

Understanding Cupping Marks

  • Circular marks are normal and expected
  • Dark marks indicate stagnation or inflammation
  • Lighter marks show better circulation
  • Marks typically fade in 3-7 days
  • Not bruises - they don't hurt
  • Color indicates body's response
  • Successive treatments show improvement

When NOT Recommended

  • Pregnancy (certain areas)
  • Open wounds or skin infections
  • Severe bleeding disorders
  • Active cancer or tumor sites
  • Severe anemia
  • Recent surgery areas
  • Sunburned or damaged skin
  • Certain skin conditions

Aftercare Tips

  • Stay hydrated (drink plenty of water)
  • Avoid cold exposure for 24 hours
  • Rest and allow body to heal
  • Light activities only for 24 hours
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours
  • Eat light, nutritious meals
  • Gentle stretching encouraged
  • Monitor marks - report concerns

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about cupping therapy

What is cupping therapy and how does it work?

+
Cupping therapy is an ancient healing practice where special cups are placed on the skin to create suction. This draws blood to the surface, promoting circulation, reducing inflammation, releasing muscle tension, and facilitating the body's natural healing processes. The suction mobilizes blood flow and breaks up stagnation in tissues.

Is cupping therapy painful?

+
Cupping therapy is generally not painful. You may feel a tight pulling sensation during the treatment, but most patients find it relaxing and therapeutic. The circular marks left behind are painless and typically fade within 3-7 days. Any discomfort is minimal and brief.

Why does cupping leave marks?

+
The circular marks are caused by blood being drawn to the surface and indicate areas of stagnation or inflammation. They're not bruises and don't hurt. Darker marks show greater stagnation, while lighter marks indicate better circulation. The marks are a positive sign that toxins and stagnant blood are being released.

How long does a cupping therapy session last?

+
A typical cupping therapy session lasts 20-30 minutes. The cups remain in place for 5-15 minutes depending on the technique used and your condition. Your first session may be slightly longer to include consultation and assessment. Treatment duration is customized based on your needs.

How many cupping sessions will I need?

+
Most patients see significant improvement within 2-4 sessions. Acute conditions may respond faster, while chronic issues might require 6-8 sessions. We typically schedule sessions 3-7 days apart. Your physiotherapist will create a personalized plan based on your condition and progress.

Can athletes benefit from cupping therapy?

+
Absolutely! Many professional athletes use cupping therapy for faster recovery, injury prevention, and performance enhancement. It reduces muscle soreness, increases range of motion, speeds healing of sports injuries, and can be used before or after competitions. Sports cupping is a specialized technique we offer.

What's the difference between dry and wet cupping?

+
Dry cupping uses suction alone without any incisions - this is what we primarily practice at PhysioNutra. Wet cupping (hijama) involves small incisions to draw out blood. At our clinic, we focus on dry cupping methods including stationary, moving, and fire cupping, which are highly effective without being invasive.

Can cupping therapy help with chronic pain?

+
Yes! Cupping therapy is highly effective for chronic pain conditions like fibromyalgia, back pain, neck pain, and arthritis. It reduces inflammation, improves circulation to affected areas, releases muscle tension, and triggers the body's natural pain-relieving mechanisms. Many patients find significant relief when combined with other physiotherapy treatments.

Is cupping therapy scientifically proven?

+
Yes, growing scientific research supports cupping therapy's effectiveness. Studies show it reduces pain, improves blood flow, decreases inflammation, and enhances muscle recovery. While more research is ongoing, evidence supports its use for musculoskeletal conditions, and it's practiced in many modern medical settings worldwide.
