What is Cupping?

Cupping Therapy: What You Need to Know Before Your First Session

Cupping therapy is an ancient healing practice used for thousands of years across Chinese, Middle Eastern, and Ayurvedic traditions. At PhysioNutra Clinic, we use specialised cups placed on the skin to create suction, drawing blood to the surface and mobilising the body's natural healing processes.

The negative pressure created by cupping releases fascial restrictions, reduces muscle tension, improves lymphatic drainage, and stimulates circulation to injured or stagnant tissue. Athletes, weekend warriors, and everyday patients across Chandigarh Tricity rely on our cupping protocols for pain relief and recovery.

Your Session

Your Cupping Therapy Journey

What to expect from first consultation to follow-up — step by step

1

Initial Consultation

Our physiotherapist assesses your condition, reviews your health history, and determines which cupping technique is most appropriate. We identify target areas and walk you through the full procedure before beginning.

2

Preparation

You are positioned comfortably on the treatment table. The skin is cleaned and, where moving cupping is used, a thin layer of oil is applied to allow the cups to glide smoothly. All equipment is sterile and single-use where applicable.

3

Cup Placement & Treatment

Cups are applied using vacuum suction or the fire method. You will feel a firm pulling sensation — not pain. Cups remain in place for 5–15 minutes, or are moved across the skin for gliding techniques. The session typically runs 20–30 minutes.

4

Cup Removal & Aftercare

Cups are gently removed, leaving circular marks that are painless and fade in 3–7 days. We apply soothing lotion if needed and provide written aftercare instructions including hydration, rest, and activity guidance for the next 24 hours.

5

Personalised Follow-Up Plan

We create a treatment schedule tailored to your condition. Most patients see improvement within 2–4 sessions. Sessions are typically spaced 3–7 days apart. We may combine cupping with dry needling, manual therapy, or exercise rehabilitation for optimal outcomes.

Safety & Guidance

Safety, Marks & Aftercare

Everything you need to know to prepare for and recover from your cupping session

Understanding Cupping Marks

  • Circular marks are normal and expected
  • Dark marks indicate tissue stagnation or inflammation
  • Lighter marks indicate better circulation
  • Marks are painless — not bruises
  • Typically fade fully in 3–7 days
  • Successive treatments show lighter marks
  • Colour indicates your body’s response

When NOT Recommended

  • Open wounds or active skin infections
  • Severe bleeding disorders or on blood thinners
  • Active cancer at treatment sites
  • Pregnancy (certain areas)
  • Severe anaemia
  • Recent surgery areas
  • Sunburned or severely damaged skin
  • Uncontrolled hypertension

Aftercare Tips

  • Drink plenty of water for 24 hours
  • Avoid cold exposure to treated areas
  • Rest and allow your body to recover
  • Light activities only for 24 hours
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours
  • Eat light, nutritious meals
  • Gentle stretching is encouraged
  • Contact us if marks persist beyond 10 days
