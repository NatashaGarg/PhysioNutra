What is Cardiac Rehabilitation?

Cardiac rehabilitation is a medically supervised program designed to help heart patients recover safely and improve cardiovascular health. At PhysioNutra Clinic, our cardiac rehab program combines monitored exercise, education, and lifestyle counseling to help you regain strength, reduce risk of future heart problems, and improve quality of life.

According to the American Heart Association, cardiac rehabilitation can reduce the risk of death from heart disease by up to 25% and significantly improve exercise capacity, symptoms, and overall wellbeing. Our program is tailored to your specific condition and recovery needs.

Why Choose Our Cardiac Rehab Program?

  • Individualized, physician-approved exercise plans
  • Continuous heart rate and vitals monitoring
  • Progressive, safe exercise progression
  • Nutrition and lifestyle counseling
  • Stress management techniques
  • Home visit options for convenience

Important Note

All cardiac rehabilitation programs require medical clearance from your cardiologist or physician before starting. We work closely with your medical team to ensure safe, effective recovery.

4 Phases of Cardiac Rehabilitation

Structured, progressive program for safe recovery

1

Phase 1: Inpatient (Acute Phase)

Hospital Stay - Days 1-7

Begins in the hospital immediately after cardiac event or surgery. Focus on early mobilization and preventing complications.

  • Bedside exercises and gentle movement
  • Education about heart condition and recovery
  • Breathing exercises and chest physiotherapy
  • Gradual increase in activity levels
  • Preparation for discharge and home care
2

Phase 2: Early Outpatient (Subacute)

Weeks 2-12 Post-Discharge

Supervised exercise program with close monitoring. Most critical phase for establishing safe exercise habits and preventing complications.

  • Monitored exercise sessions 2-3 times per week
  • Heart rate and blood pressure monitoring
  • Progressive cardiovascular conditioning
  • Strength training with light resistance
  • Education on risk factor modification
  • Nutritional counseling and meal planning
3

Phase 3: Intermediate Outpatient

Months 3-6

Transition to more independent exercise with periodic supervision. Building confidence and establishing long-term exercise habits.

  • Reduced supervision frequency
  • Advanced exercise progression
  • Home exercise program development
  • Stress testing to assess progress
  • Continued lifestyle modification support
4

Phase 4: Maintenance (Long-term)

6+ Months Ongoing

Long-term maintenance program focusing on sustaining healthy lifestyle and preventing future cardiac events.

  • Independent, self-directed exercise
  • Periodic check-ins and assessments
  • Continued heart-healthy lifestyle
  • Community exercise program participation
  • Ongoing monitoring of risk factors

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about cardiac rehabilitation

Is cardiac rehabilitation safe?

+
Yes, cardiac rehabilitation is very safe when properly supervised. According to the National Institutes of Health, serious complications during cardiac rehab are extremely rare (less than 1 in 60,000 sessions). All exercises are carefully monitored, and our staff is trained in cardiac emergency procedures. We continuously monitor your heart rate, blood pressure, and symptoms to ensure safety.

When should I start cardiac rehabilitation?

+
Cardiac rehabilitation typically begins 1-2 weeks after hospital discharge following a heart attack, surgery, or cardiac procedure. However, the exact timing depends on your specific condition and your doctor's recommendations. Early participation (within 2-6 weeks) is associated with better outcomes. Always get medical clearance from your cardiologist before starting any exercise program.

How long does cardiac rehabilitation take?

+
Phase 2 (supervised outpatient rehab) typically lasts 8-12 weeks with 2-3 sessions per week. Each session is approximately 1 hour. Phase 3 continues for 3-6 months with reduced supervision. Phase 4 is ongoing maintenance that continues indefinitely. The total duration varies based on individual progress, goals, and medical condition. Most patients see significant improvements within 3 months.

What exercises are included in cardiac rehabilitation?

+
Exercises include supervised aerobic activities like treadmill walking, stationary cycling, arm ergometry, and low-impact cardio exercises. We also include light resistance training for major muscle groups, flexibility exercises, and stretching. All exercises are individualized based on your fitness level, cardiac condition, and specific limitations. Exercise intensity is carefully controlled and progressively increased as your fitness improves.

Can I do cardiac rehabilitation at home?

+
Yes, we offer home-based cardiac rehabilitation for patients who cannot easily travel to the clinic. Home programs include initial assessment, personalized exercise plans, remote monitoring guidance, regular follow-up visits, and telehealth check-ins. However, clinic-based programs with direct supervision are generally preferred, especially in the early phases (first 8-12 weeks) when close monitoring is most beneficial. We can customize a program that combines both clinic and home sessions.

Will cardiac rehab help me feel better?

+
Absolutely! Most patients report significant improvements in energy levels, reduced shortness of breath, better sleep, decreased anxiety and depression, improved confidence in daily activities, and enhanced overall quality of life. Studies show that 85-90% of cardiac rehab participants report feeling better and more confident after completing the program. Many patients are able to return to work, hobbies, and activities they thought they'd never do again.

Is cardiac rehabilitation covered by insurance?

+
Many insurance plans in India cover cardiac rehabilitation when prescribed by a physician. Coverage varies by policy and provider. We recommend checking with your insurance company about coverage for cardiac rehab services. We provide detailed documentation and invoices that can be submitted for reimbursement. Even if not fully covered, the health benefits and potential cost savings from preventing future cardiac events make cardiac rehab a valuable investment in your health.
