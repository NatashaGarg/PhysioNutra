What is Cardiac Rehab?

Cardiac Rehabilitation: What You Need to Know Before Starting

Cardiac rehabilitation is a medically supervised programme designed to help heart patients recover safely, rebuild strength, and reduce the risk of future cardiac events. It combines monitored exercise, lifestyle counselling, nutritional guidance, and psychological support into a structured, phase-based recovery pathway.

At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg guides every heart patient through an individualised programme aligned with AHA, ESC, and Indian Heart Association guidelines. Whether you are recovering from a heart attack, bypass surgery, angioplasty, or managing chronic heart failure, our team is here to help you return to full, confident living.

Recovery Roadmap

4 Phases of Cardiac Rehabilitation

A structured, progressive programme guided by medical evidence and objective milestones

1

Phase 1 — Inpatient (Acute Phase)

Hospital Stay · Days 1–7

Begins in the hospital immediately after cardiac event or surgery. Focus on early mobilisation and preventing post-operative complications.

  • Bedside exercises and gentle movement
  • Breathing exercises and chest physiotherapy
  • Education about your heart condition and recovery
  • Gradual increase in activity levels within hospital
  • Preparation for discharge and home care planning
2

Phase 2 — Early Outpatient (Subacute)

Weeks 2–12 Post-Discharge

Supervised exercise programme with continuous monitoring. The most critical phase for building safe exercise habits and preventing re-hospitalisation.

  • Monitored sessions 2–3 times per week
  • Continuous heart rate and blood pressure monitoring
  • Progressive cardiovascular conditioning (treadmill, cycling)
  • Light resistance training for muscle strength
  • Nutritional counselling and heart-healthy meal planning
  • Education on risk factor modification (smoking, cholesterol, BP)
3

Phase 3 — Intermediate Outpatient

Months 3–6

Transition to more independent exercise with periodic supervision. Building long-term confidence, stamina, and healthy lifestyle habits.

  • Reduced supervision frequency with home exercise integration
  • Advanced progressive exercise programme
  • Stress management and relaxation techniques
  • Return-to-work and daily activity planning
  • Continued lifestyle modification and psychosocial support
4

Phase 4 — Long-Term Maintenance

6+ Months · Ongoing

Long-term maintenance programme focused on sustaining heart health and preventing future cardiac events for life.

  • Independent, self-directed exercise programme
  • Periodic reassessment and plan updates
  • Community exercise participation guidance
  • Ongoing monitoring of cardiovascular risk factors
  • Open access for queries and follow-up consultations
Our Programmes

Comprehensive Cardiac Rehabilitation Components

Every programme is personalised, medically approved, and supervised by Dr. Tarun Garg

Supervised Exercise Training

Individualised cardiovascular and strength training sessions with continuous heart rate and blood pressure monitoring. Includes treadmill walking, stationary cycling, arm ergometry, and progressive resistance work tailored to your condition.

Nutritional Counselling

Heart-healthy eating plans, weight management strategies, and dietary modifications to lower cholesterol, blood pressure, and blood sugar. Our integrated nutritional approach supports both physical recovery and long-term cardiovascular risk reduction.

Stress Management

Relaxation techniques, mindfulness-based breathing, and cognitive coping strategies to reduce cardiac stress load. Psychological wellbeing is a key determinant of long-term cardiac outcomes and quality of life.

Patient Education

Comprehensive education on your specific cardiac condition, medications, warning signs, activity guidelines, and self-monitoring techniques. Empowering you with knowledge is central to lifelong heart health management.

Home-Based Cardiac Rehab

For patients who cannot easily travel to the clinic, our therapists provide monitored home sessions with professional equipment. Ideal during early recovery phases and for elderly patients. Available across the Tricity region.

Psychosocial Support

Structured support for anxiety, depression, and fear of activity — conditions affecting up to 40% of post-cardiac patients. We integrate psychological readiness tools and confidence-building strategies throughout all phases.
