What is Electrotherapy?

Electrotherapy is an advanced physiotherapy treatment that uses electrical energy for therapeutic purposes. At PhysioNutra Clinic, we utilize state-of-the-art electrotherapy equipment to deliver targeted electrical stimulation that reduces pain, promotes healing, improves circulation, and strengthens muscles.

Our certified physiotherapists are trained in multiple electrotherapy modalities including TENS (Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation), IFT (Interferential Therapy), therapeutic ultrasound, LASER therapy, and electrical muscle stimulation. Each treatment is customized to your specific condition for optimal results.

  • Non-invasive and drug-free pain management
  • Accelerates natural healing processes
  • Reduces inflammation and swelling
  • Improves blood circulation to injured areas

Our Electrotherapy Modalities

Advanced equipment and expert techniques for comprehensive treatment

TENS Therapy

Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation

TENS therapy uses low-voltage electrical currents to provide pain relief by blocking pain signals to the brain and stimulating endorphin production.

  • Effective for chronic and acute pain
  • Non-addictive pain management
  • Can be used during daily activities
  • Ideal for back pain, arthritis, nerve pain

IFT Therapy

Interferential Therapy

IFT uses medium-frequency electrical currents that penetrate deeper into tissues, providing effective pain relief and promoting healing.

  • Deep tissue penetration
  • Reduces inflammation and swelling
  • Improves blood circulation
  • Effective for sports injuries and joint pain

Ultrasound Therapy

Therapeutic Ultrasound

Uses high-frequency sound waves to generate heat deep within tissues, promoting healing and reducing pain and muscle spasms.

  • Accelerates tissue repair
  • Increases blood flow to injured areas
  • Reduces scar tissue formation
  • Ideal for tendonitis and ligament injuries

LASER Therapy

Low-Level Laser Therapy (LLLT)

Cold laser therapy uses specific wavelengths of light to interact with tissue, accelerating healing and reducing pain and inflammation.

  • Non-thermal and painless treatment
  • Stimulates cellular regeneration
  • Reduces inflammation significantly
  • Effective for wound healing and nerve pain

EMS Therapy

Electrical Muscle Stimulation

EMS uses electrical impulses to cause muscles to contract, preventing muscle atrophy and strengthening weakened muscles.

  • Prevents muscle wasting
  • Strengthens weak muscles
  • Improves muscle tone and function
  • Essential for post-surgical recovery

Heat & Cold Therapy

Thermal Electrotherapy

Combines electrical stimulation with controlled heating or cooling for enhanced therapeutic effects on muscles and joints.

  • Reduces muscle tension and stiffness
  • Controls acute inflammation
  • Enhances muscle relaxation
  • Complementary to other treatments

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about electrotherapy

What is electrotherapy in physiotherapy?

+
Electrotherapy uses electrical energy for therapeutic purposes to reduce pain, stimulate muscles, improve circulation, and accelerate healing. Common modalities include TENS, IFT, ultrasound therapy, LASER therapy, and electrical muscle stimulation. Each treatment delivers controlled electrical currents tailored to your specific condition.

Is electrotherapy safe and painful?

+
Electrotherapy is very safe when administered by qualified physiotherapists. Most treatments are painless - you'll feel mild tingling, warmth, or gentle muscle contractions depending on the modality used. The intensity is always adjusted to your comfort level. Some people find it quite relaxing.

How many electrotherapy sessions are needed?

+
Treatment duration varies by condition and severity. Acute pain may improve in 3-5 sessions, while chronic conditions typically require 8-12 sessions for optimal results. Many patients notice improvement after the first few sessions. Your physiotherapist will create a personalized plan and adjust based on your progress.

What conditions can electrotherapy treat?

+
Electrotherapy effectively treats back pain, neck pain, arthritis, sciatica, sports injuries, muscle strains, frozen shoulder, tendonitis, nerve pain, post-surgical pain, fibromyalgia, and many other musculoskeletal conditions. It's particularly effective for both acute injuries and chronic pain management.

How long does each electrotherapy session last?

+
Session duration varies by modality. TENS and IFT typically last 20-30 minutes, ultrasound 5-10 minutes, LASER therapy 5-15 minutes, and EMS 15-25 minutes. Your physiotherapist may combine multiple modalities or include manual therapy and exercises for comprehensive treatment.

What's the difference between TENS and IFT therapy?

+
TENS uses low-frequency currents primarily for pain relief at the skin surface, while IFT uses medium-frequency currents that penetrate deeper into tissues. IFT is better for deep tissue pain and inflammation, while TENS excels at superficial pain relief and nerve pain. Your physiotherapist will choose based on your condition.

Are there any side effects of electrotherapy?

+
Electrotherapy has minimal side effects when properly administered. Some patients may experience mild skin redness at electrode sites or temporary muscle soreness, which resolves quickly. Electrotherapy is contraindicated for people with pacemakers, pregnancy, cancer, epilepsy, or over certain areas. Your therapist will screen for contraindications.

Can I combine electrotherapy with other treatments?

+
Yes! Electrotherapy is often combined with manual therapy, exercise therapy, and other physiotherapy modalities for comprehensive treatment. This multimodal approach typically produces better results than electrotherapy alone. Your physiotherapist will create an integrated treatment plan tailored to your needs.
