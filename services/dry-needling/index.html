What is Dry Needling?

Dry Needling: What You Need to Know Before Your First Session

Dry needling is a Western medicine-based therapeutic technique where thin sterile needles are inserted directly into myofascial trigger points — the tight, hypersensitive bands within muscle tissue commonly called “knots”. The needle elicits a local twitch response that resets the trigger point, restores normal muscle function, and initiates a healing cascade.

At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg applies dry needling as part of a comprehensive physiotherapy plan — not as a standalone procedure. It is combined with manual therapy, exercise rehabilitation, and patient education for lasting results across chronic pain, sports injuries, and post-surgical recovery.

Common Conditions

Signs dry needling may help you

  • Persistent muscle knots or tightness that won’t release with massage
  • Chronic neck, shoulder, or back pain unresolved by other treatments
  • Referred pain patterns — aching radiating from one area to another
  • Sports injuries with localised muscle dysfunction or spasm
  • Tension headaches or jaw pain originating from tight neck muscles
  • Reduced range of motion due to muscle guarding or stiffness

 Dry needling is often most effective when combined with manual therapy and progressive exercise. Book a free assessment — we will identify whether dry needling is appropriate for your condition.

Your Session

Your Dry Needling Treatment Journey

What to expect from assessment to follow-up — step by step

1

Comprehensive Assessment

Our physiotherapist evaluates your posture, movement patterns, and trigger point locations. We review your pain history and treatment goals to confirm dry needling is the right approach and identify target muscles.

2

Treatment Planning & Consent

We walk you through the procedure, explain what the local twitch response feels like, and answer all questions. You provide informed consent and we confirm there are no contraindications to treatment.

3

Needle Insertion

Thin, sterile single-use needles are precisely inserted into identified trigger points. You may feel a brief pinch at entry, then a deep aching or muscle twitch — the local twitch response — indicating the trigger point has been engaged. This is normal and therapeutic.

4

Treatment & Needle Duration

Needles remain in place for 5–10 minutes while you rest. Your physiotherapist may use gentle piston technique to maximise trigger point release. A typical session treats 3–8 trigger points per visit.

5

Post-Treatment Care

After needle removal, heat or ice may be applied and stretching exercises prescribed. You will receive written aftercare instructions. Mild post-treatment soreness (like DOMS) for 24–48 hours is normal and resolves spontaneously.

6

Follow-Up & Progress Monitoring

We reassess at each visit and adjust the treatment plan based on your response. Most patients need 3–6 sessions, scheduled 3–7 days apart, for optimal results. We always combine dry needling with exercise therapy for lasting outcomes.
