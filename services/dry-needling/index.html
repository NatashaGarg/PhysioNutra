What is Dry Needling?

Dry needling uses thin sterile needles inserted into trigger points (muscle knots) to release tension, reduce pain, and restore muscle function. Certified PhysioNutra therapists target the exact source of musculoskeletal pain for fast relief.

Best for: chronic back/neck pain, sports injuries, tension headaches, sciatica. 3-6 sessions typically resolve acute issues. Available in Zirakpur clinic + home visits (Chandigarh, Mohali, Panchkula).

Your Dry Needling Treatment Journey

What to expect during your session at PhysioNutra Clinic

1

Comprehensive Assessment

Our physiotherapist evaluates your condition, identifies trigger points, assesses movement patterns, and discusses your pain history to create a personalized treatment plan.

2

Treatment Planning

We explain the dry needling process, answer your questions, and ensure you're comfortable. You'll understand what to expect during and after treatment.

3

Needle Insertion

Thin, sterile needles are precisely inserted into identified trigger points. You may feel a brief pinch followed by a muscle twitch (called a "local twitch response") - this is normal and therapeutic.

4

Treatment Duration

Needles remain in place for 5-10 minutes while you relax. Some techniques involve gentle manipulation of the needle to maximize trigger point release.

5

Post-Treatment Care

After needle removal, we may apply heat or ice and provide stretching exercises. You'll receive home care instructions to maximize treatment benefits.

6

Follow-Up & Progress

We monitor your response and adjust treatment frequency. Most patients need 3-6 sessions for optimal results, scheduled 3-7 days apart.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about dry needling therapy

What is dry needling and how does it work?

+
Dry needling is a therapeutic technique where thin needles are inserted into trigger points (muscle knots) to release tension, reduce pain, and improve muscle function. It works by stimulating the body's natural healing response, increasing blood flow, and releasing tight muscle bands. The technique is based on Western medical principles and directly targets musculoskeletal issues.

Is dry needling painful?

+
Most patients experience minimal discomfort during dry needling. You may feel a brief pinch when the needle is inserted, followed by a muscle twitch or deep aching sensation when the trigger point is hit. This is normal and actually indicates the treatment is working. Many patients find the overall experience relaxing and the relief worth any brief discomfort.

How is dry needling different from acupuncture?

+
While both use thin needles, dry needling is based on Western medical principles and targets specific trigger points and muscle tissue for pain relief. Acupuncture is based on Traditional Chinese Medicine and focuses on balancing energy meridians. Dry needling is performed by trained physiotherapists specifically for musculoskeletal conditions, with needles often inserted deeper into the muscle tissue.

How many dry needling sessions will I need?

+
The number of sessions varies based on your condition. Most patients see significant improvement within 3-6 sessions scheduled 3-7 days apart. Acute conditions may respond faster (1-3 sessions), while chronic issues might require more treatments (6-10 sessions). Your physiotherapist will create a personalized plan and adjust based on your progress.

What should I expect after dry needling treatment?

+
After treatment, you may experience mild soreness (similar to post-workout soreness) for 24-48 hours. This is normal and indicates your body is healing. Some patients feel immediate relief, while others notice gradual improvement over a few days. Staying hydrated, gentle stretching, and applying heat can help reduce post-treatment soreness. Most patients can resume normal activities immediately.

Are there any side effects or risks?

+
Dry needling is very safe when performed by trained professionals. Common minor side effects include temporary soreness, bruising at needle sites, or mild fatigue. Serious complications are extremely rare. At PhysioNutra, we use sterile, single-use needles and follow strict safety protocols. The treatment is contraindicated for pregnant women, people with bleeding disorders, or those with extreme needle phobia.

Can dry needling help with chronic pain?

+
Yes! Dry needling is highly effective for chronic pain conditions like persistent back pain, neck pain, fibromyalgia, and myofascial pain syndrome. It addresses the underlying muscle dysfunction and trigger points that contribute to chronic pain. When combined with other physiotherapy treatments and exercises, it can provide significant long-term relief for chronic pain sufferers.

Is dry needling covered by insurance?

+
Coverage varies by insurance provider and plan. Some insurance companies cover dry needling as part of physiotherapy treatment, while others may not. We recommend checking with your insurance provider about coverage. Our team can provide detailed invoices and treatment documentation to help with insurance claims or reimbursement requests.

Who performs dry needling at PhysioNutra?

+
Dry needling at PhysioNutra is performed exclusively by our certified physiotherapists who have completed specialized training and certification in dry needling techniques. Our team has extensive experience treating musculoskeletal conditions and follows strict safety and hygiene protocols. You can trust you're in expert, professional hands.
