What is Women's Health Physiotherapy?

Women's health physiotherapy is a specialized area of physiotherapy focusing on conditions unique to women throughout their life stages. At PhysioNutra Clinic, our trained women's health physiotherapists provide evidence-based treatment for pelvic floor dysfunction, pregnancy-related issues, postpartum recovery, urinary incontinence, pelvic pain, and more in a comfortable, confidential setting.

Whether you're preparing for pregnancy, recovering after childbirth, experiencing bladder control issues, or dealing with pelvic pain, our comprehensive approach addresses your concerns with sensitivity and expertise. We understand that many women's health issues are personal and often go untreated due to embarrassment, but these conditions are very common and highly treatable with proper physiotherapy.

üî¨ Evidence-Based Women's Health Care

Pelvic floor physiotherapy is strongly supported by scientific research. A comprehensive review published in the National Institutes of Health (NIH) confirms that pelvic floor muscle training is highly effective for treating stress urinary incontinence, with success rates of 60-80% in women who follow prescribed exercise programs.

Furthermore, research published in the American Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology demonstrates that women's health physiotherapy during pregnancy and postpartum significantly reduces the risk of urinary incontinence, pelvic pain, and diastasis recti, while accelerating recovery and improving overall quality of life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about women's health physiotherapy

What is women's health physiotherapy?

+
Women's health physiotherapy is a specialized area focusing on conditions unique to women, including pelvic floor dysfunction, pregnancy-related issues, postpartum recovery, urinary incontinence, pelvic pain, and pelvic organ prolapse. Our trained physiotherapists provide evidence-based treatments in a comfortable, confidential environment using exercises, manual therapy, biofeedback, and education.

What is pelvic floor physiotherapy?

+
Pelvic floor physiotherapy involves assessment and treatment of the muscles, ligaments, and connective tissues that support your pelvic organs (bladder, uterus, rectum). Treatment includes specialized exercises (Kegels), manual therapy techniques, biofeedback, and lifestyle education to treat conditions like incontinence, prolapse, and pelvic pain. It's a non-surgical, medication-free approach that's highly effective.

When should I start pregnancy physiotherapy?

+
You can start pregnancy physiotherapy at any stage, but early intervention (first trimester) is ideal for preventing common issues like back pain and pelvic pain. If you're already experiencing discomfort, start immediately ‚Äì it's never too late. We also recommend pre-conception physiotherapy to strengthen your pelvic floor and core before pregnancy. Treatment is safe throughout all trimesters when performed by a trained women's health physiotherapist.

How soon after delivery can I start physiotherapy?

+
Gentle pelvic floor and breathing exercises can start within days of delivery. We recommend a comprehensive assessment at 6 weeks postpartum (or once cleared by your doctor after C-section). However, if you're experiencing significant pain, leakage, or other concerns, contact us earlier. Early intervention leads to better outcomes. Don't wait ‚Äì postpartum physiotherapy is essential for proper recovery, whether you had a vaginal birth or C-section.

Is urinary incontinence normal after childbirth?

+
While urinary incontinence is common after childbirth (affecting 30-50% of women), it's NOT normal and should NOT be ignored. Many women are told it's "just part of motherhood," but this is false. Pelvic floor physiotherapy has 60-80% success rates in treating postpartum incontinence. The earlier you address it, the better the outcomes. Don't suffer in silence ‚Äì treatment is available and highly effective!

What should I expect during my first appointment?

+
Your first appointment includes a detailed discussion of your symptoms, medical history, pregnancy/birth history, and goals. We'll perform an external assessment of your posture, movement, and breathing. If appropriate and with your consent, an internal pelvic floor assessment may be recommended to evaluate muscle strength, tone, and coordination. This is optional and only done if you're comfortable. We'll then create a personalized treatment plan. Sessions are conducted privately in a comfortable, judgment-free environment.

Do you have female physiotherapists?

+
Yes! We understand that many women prefer treatment from female physiotherapists for women's health concerns. PhysioNutra Clinic has specially trained female physiotherapists who provide all women's health and pelvic floor services. Your comfort and privacy are our top priorities. We also offer home visits if you prefer treatment in your own environment.

How long does treatment take?

+
Duration varies based on your condition. Mild incontinence may improve in 4-8 weeks with consistent exercise. More complex issues like prolapse or chronic pelvic pain may require 3-6 months. Postpartum recovery typically involves 6-12 weeks of treatment. We typically recommend sessions 1-2 times weekly initially, reducing frequency as you progress. Most patients see significant improvement within 6-8 weeks. Consistency with home exercises is key to success!

Note: Our women's health physiotherapists are specially certified in pelvic floor rehabilitation and stay current with the latest evidence-based practices through continuous professional development.

Is women's health physiotherapy covered by insurance?

+
Coverage varies by insurance provider and plan. Many insurance policies cover physiotherapy services, including women's health physiotherapy, especially when medically necessary. We recommend checking with your insurance provider about specific coverage for pelvic floor therapy and women's health services. Our team can provide detailed invoices and treatment documentation to support your insurance claims or reimbursement requests.
