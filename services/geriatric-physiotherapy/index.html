What is Geriatric Physiotherapy?

Specialised Physiotherapy Designed for Elderly Patients

Geriatric physiotherapy is specialised physical therapy for older adults aged 60 and above, addressing the unique physical challenges of ageing — from arthritis and osteoporosis to stroke recovery and fall prevention. At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg provides compassionate, evidence-based elderly care with a strong focus on maintaining independence, dignity, and quality of life.

Growing older does not mean accepting pain, limited mobility, or loss of independence. With the right physiotherapy programme — delivered at home or at our clinic — most elderly patients make meaningful improvements in strength, balance, and day-to-day function.

Who Benefits Most?

Signs your elderly family member needs physiotherapy

  • Frequent falls or fear of falling while walking
  • Joint pain, stiffness, or swelling from arthritis
  • Difficulty rising from a chair or climbing stairs
  • Weakness and reduced mobility after stroke or surgery
  • Tremors, rigidity, or walking changes in Parkinson’s
  • Bedridden or largely immobile due to illness or injury
  • General weakness and deconditioning from inactivity

 Physiotherapy is safe and beneficial at any age. Research shows targeted elderly physiotherapy reduces fall risk by up to 40% and significantly improves quality of life and independence.

Conditions Treated

Age-Related Conditions We Treat

Specialised programmes for the most common and complex elderly health conditions

Arthritis & Joint Pain

Osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis cause significant pain, stiffness, and functional limitation in elderly patients. Our programme restores movement and reduces pain without medication.

  • Gentle joint mobilisation and manual therapy
  • Range of motion and strengthening exercises
  • Joint protection strategies
  • Hydrotherapy-based exercises where indicated
  • Assistive device assessment and training

Balance & Gait Disorders

Poor balance and walking difficulties are leading causes of falls and reduced confidence in older adults. Our fall prevention programme addresses all contributing factors.

  • Progressive balance and coordination training
  • Gait pattern assessment and correction
  • Vestibular rehabilitation for dizziness
  • Walking aid recommendation and training
  • Home safety assessment and modifications

Stroke Rehabilitation

Post-stroke physiotherapy helps seniors regain function, mobility, and independence following cerebrovascular events, with specialised neurological rehabilitation techniques.

  • Neuromuscular re-education and muscle activation
  • Mobility and transfers training
  • Spasticity management
  • Activities of daily living (ADL) training
  • Caregiver and family education

Post-Surgical Recovery

Elderly patients need specialised rehabilitation after hip or knee replacement, fracture surgery, or cardiac procedures to ensure safe, effective, and complication-free recovery.

  • Hip and knee replacement rehabilitation
  • Fracture and orthopaedic recovery
  • Progressive strength and mobility restoration
  • Complication and bedsore prevention
  • Safe return to independent function

Parkinson’s Disease

Specialised exercise programmes and movement training help manage Parkinson’s symptoms, maintain functional abilities, and reduce fall risk in elderly patients.

  • Rigidity reduction and flexibility training
  • Tremor management strategies
  • Gait and cueing techniques
  • Posture and balance rehabilitation
  • Falls prevention protocols

General Weakness & Deconditioning

Age-related muscle loss (sarcopenia) and prolonged inactivity cause weakness and reduced endurance, but both are reversible with properly prescribed exercise therapy.

  • Progressive resistance and strength training
  • Cardiovascular endurance building
  • Flexibility and range of motion work
  • Functional capacity restoration
  • Energy conservation and activity pacing
