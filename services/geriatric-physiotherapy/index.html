Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about geriatric physiotherapy

What is geriatric physiotherapy?

+
Geriatric physiotherapy is specialized physical therapy designed for elderly individuals aged 65 and above. It focuses on age-related conditions like arthritis, osteoporosis, balance disorders, post-stroke recovery, and mobility issues. The treatment aims to improve independence, reduce fall risk, manage pain, enhance quality of life, and maintain functional abilities through gentle exercises, balance training, and functional rehabilitation tailored to seniors' unique needs and capabilities.

Can physiotherapy help elderly people at home?

+
Yes! Home-based geriatric physiotherapy is highly effective and often preferred for elderly patients. Our physiotherapists visit your home with portable equipment, assess your living environment for safety, provide personalized treatment in comfortable, familiar surroundings, and involve family members in care. This is especially beneficial for seniors with mobility limitations, those recovering from illness or surgery, or patients who feel more comfortable at home. Studies show excellent outcomes with home-based elderly care.

How can physiotherapy prevent falls in elderly?

+
Physiotherapy prevents falls through comprehensive approaches including balance and coordination exercises, strength training for leg muscles (especially quadriceps and ankles), gait training and walking practice, home safety assessments and modifications, mobility aid recommendations and training, education on fall risk factors, and vestibular rehabilitation if needed. Research from the National Institutes of Health shows that targeted physiotherapy can reduce fall risk by up to 40% in elderly individuals.

What conditions do geriatric physiotherapists treat?

+
Geriatric physiotherapists treat a wide range of age-related conditions including arthritis pain and stiffness (osteoarthritis, rheumatoid), osteoporosis management, stroke recovery and rehabilitation, Parkinson's disease symptoms, balance and gait disorders, post-surgical rehabilitation (hip/knee replacements), chronic pain conditions, joint replacements recovery, fracture healing, dementia-related mobility issues, general age-related weakness and deconditioning, cardiovascular conditions, and respiratory problems like COPD.

Is physiotherapy safe for very elderly patients?

+
Yes, geriatric physiotherapy is specifically designed to be safe for elderly patients of all ages, even those in their 80s, 90s, or 100+. Treatments are gentle, carefully progressed, adapted to individual capabilities and medical conditions, monitored closely for any adverse responses, and modified based on daily energy levels and health status. Our physiotherapists are specially trained in senior care and work closely with families and physicians to ensure safe, effective treatment. Exercise is beneficial at any age when properly prescribed.

How long does geriatric physiotherapy take to show results?

+
Results vary based on condition and consistency, but many elderly patients notice improvements within 2-4 weeks of regular treatment. Pain relief may occur sooner (within days to weeks), balance improvements typically show in 3-6 weeks, strength gains appear in 4-8 weeks, and functional mobility improvements develop over 6-12 weeks. Consistency is key - attending sessions regularly and performing home exercises significantly accelerates progress. Even small improvements can dramatically impact quality of life and independence for seniors.

Do you provide physiotherapy for bedridden elderly patients?

+
Yes, we provide specialized care for bedridden elderly patients. Treatment includes passive range of motion exercises to prevent joint stiffness, chest physiotherapy for respiratory health, positioning advice to prevent bedsores, gentle strengthening when appropriate, transfers and mobility training, and caregiver education on proper techniques. Our goal is to maximize comfort, prevent complications, maintain existing function, and when possible, work toward sitting and eventual standing. We adapt all treatments to the patient's current condition and capabilities.
