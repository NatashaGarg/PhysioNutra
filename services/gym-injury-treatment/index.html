Warning Signs

Signs you need professional treatment

  • Sharp, sudden pain during or after exercise
  • Swelling or bruising around a joint or muscle
  • Pain that persists more than 48–72 hours after training
  • Limited range of motion or weakness in a limb
  • A “pop” or “snap” sensation at the time of injury
  • Pain that worsens with continued training

Conditions We Treat

Common Gym Injuries We Treat

Specialist physiotherapy for every fitness-related injury across Chandigarh Tricity

Shoulder Injuries

Most common in weightlifters, from bench press and overhead movements.

  • Rotator cuff tears
  • Shoulder impingement
  • AC joint injuries
  • Bicep tendonitis
  • SLAP labrum tears

Lower Back Pain

Commonly caused by deadlifts, squats, and poor core stability during lifts.

  • Muscle and ligament strains
  • Herniated / bulging discs
  • Sciatica from heavy lifting
  • SI joint dysfunction
  • Lumbar facet joint pain

Knee Injuries

Common from squats, lunges, leg press, and running programmes.

  • Patellar tendonitis (jumper’s knee)
  • Meniscus tears
  • ACL / MCL sprains
  • IT band syndrome
  • Patellofemoral pain syndrome

Elbow & Wrist Pain

Result of repetitive movements and heavy gripping during training.

  • Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis)
  • Golfer’s elbow (medial epicondylitis)
  • Wrist sprains and tendonitis
  • Carpal tunnel syndrome
  • De Quervain’s tenosynovitis

Muscle Strains & Tears

Acute tears or chronic overuse injuries from training too hard too fast.

  • Hamstring strains (Grade I–III)
  • Pectoral muscle tears
  • Calf strains
  • Hip flexor injuries
  • Quadriceps muscle tears

Ankle & Foot Injuries

From running, jumping, and improper footwear during training.

  • Ankle sprains (lateral/medial)
  • Achilles tendonitis / tendinopathy
  • Plantar fasciitis
  • Shin splints
  • Peroneal tendon injuries

Stay Injury-Free

Preventing Gym Injuries

Expert guidance to keep you training harder and longer without setbacks

1

Proper Warm-Up

10–15 minutes of dynamic movement preparation before heavy lifting primes muscles, joints, and the nervous system for load.

2

Master Form First

Perfect technique with lighter weights before progressively adding load. Poor biomechanics are responsible for the majority of gym injuries.

3

Progressive Overload

Increase training volume or weight gradually (5–10% per week maximum). Sudden jumps in load overwhelm unprepared tissues.

4

Adequate Recovery

Allow muscle groups 48–72 hours between heavy sessions. Overtraining degrades tissue quality and significantly increases injury risk.

5

Core Stability

A strong, responsive core protects the spine during all compound lifts. Core training should be a priority, not an afterthought.

6

Listen to Your Body

Pain is a warning signal, not a challenge to push through. Sharp or persistent pain during training always warrants professional assessment.

Recovery Roadmap

Your Gym Injury Recovery Journey

A structured, phase-by-phase approach designed to get you back stronger, not just pain-free

1

Initial Assessment (Day 1)

Comprehensive evaluation including injury history, movement screening, strength testing, and a review of your training programme. We identify the root cause — not just the symptom — and build your personalised recovery plan with clear milestones.

2

Phase 1 — Acute Care (Weeks 1–2)

Goal: reduce pain, control swelling, and protect injured tissue. Manual therapy, dry needling, laser, and therapeutic modalities combined with modified training for unaffected body parts to maintain fitness and motivation.

3

Phase 2 — Restore Movement (Weeks 3–4)

Progressively restore full range of motion through targeted stretching, mobilisation, and early loading. Pain significantly reduces. Light resistance training reintroduced under supervision with corrected mechanics.

4

Phase 3 — Strengthening (Weeks 5–8)

Progressive resistance training to rebuild strength and tissue capacity. Sport-specific loading patterns introduced. Movement faults corrected with hands-on coaching. Strength symmetry targets established and tracked objectively.

5

Phase 4 — Return to Full Training (Weeks 9–12)

Gradual return to your full training programme with criteria-based clearance. Technique corrections embedded. Form checks for all key lifts. Progressive load increases with ongoing monitoring.

6

Long-Term Prevention (Ongoing)

Personalised injury prevention plan, periodic reassessment, and open access for any concerns. Our goal is your long-term athletic health — not a single episode of care.
