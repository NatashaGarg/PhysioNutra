What is Geriatric Physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy for Senior Citizens: What Families Should Know

Ageing brings unique physical challenges — stiffer joints, slower reflexes, weaker bones, and a higher risk of falls. Geriatric physiotherapy addresses these changes directly, helping elderly patients stay mobile, independent, and confident in daily life.

At PhysioNutra Clinic, Dr. Tarun Garg and his team provide gentle, evidence-based care tailored to each senior’s abilities and goals — whether that means recovering from surgery, managing a chronic condition like arthritis, or simply staying strong and steady on their feet.

When to Seek Help

Signs your elderly parent may need physiotherapy

  • Frequent stumbling, unsteadiness, or recent falls
  • Difficulty rising from a chair or climbing stairs
  • Joint stiffness or pain limiting daily activities
  • Reduced walking distance, speed, or confidence
  • Fear of falling that limits outings or independence
  • New weakness after illness, surgery, or a hospital stay

 Early intervention makes the biggest difference in preventing falls and preserving independence. Book an assessment for your loved one today.

Our Approach

Our Treatment Approach for Seniors

Compassionate, personalised, step-by-step care for every senior citizen

1

Initial Assessment

A comprehensive evaluation of medical history, current condition, mobility, balance, strength, and daily living activities — along with a home safety and fall-risk review where relevant.

2

Personalised Care Plan

Based on the assessment, we set customised treatment goals focused on the patient’s specific needs, abilities, and desired outcomes. Family input is welcomed and valued throughout.

3

Gentle Treatment Sessions

Safe, age-appropriate exercises and therapies delivered at a comfortable pace. Sessions typically run 45–60 minutes and can take place at home or at the clinic.

4

Family & Caregiver Education

We teach family members and caregivers how to assist with exercises, transfers, and daily activities safely and effectively between sessions.

5

Progress Monitoring

Regular reassessment of mobility, pain levels, and function. The treatment plan is adjusted as progress is made or needs change.

6

Long-Term Wellness

Maintenance guidance to sustain improvements and help prevent decline, with ongoing support available as needs evolve over time.
