Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about senior citizen physiotherapy

What is geriatric physiotherapy for senior citizens?

+
Geriatric physiotherapy is specialized care for elderly individuals focusing on age-related conditions like arthritis, balance issues, fall prevention, post-stroke recovery, and mobility improvement. It helps seniors maintain independence, reduce pain, and enhance quality of life through safe, evidence-based treatments tailored to their abilities.

Do you provide home physiotherapy for elderly patients?

+
Yes, we offer comprehensive home physiotherapy services across Chandigarh, Mohali, Panchkula and Zirakpur. Our physiotherapists visit elderly patients at home with all necessary equipment, making treatment convenient and comfortable. Home visits are ideal for seniors with mobility challenges or post-surgery patients.

How can physiotherapy help prevent falls in elderly?

+
Physiotherapy improves balance through specific exercises, strengthens leg muscles, enhances coordination, and teaches safe movement techniques. We assess fall risks in the home environment and provide recommendations for modifications. Studies show physiotherapy can reduce fall risk by 40-60% in elderly patients.

What conditions do you treat in senior citizens?

+
We treat arthritis, osteoporosis, post-stroke paralysis, Parkinson's disease, post-joint replacement surgery, fractures, balance disorders, chronic pain, general weakness, vertigo, respiratory issues, and post-COVID weakness. We also provide preventive care to maintain mobility and independence in healthy seniors.

Is physiotherapy safe for elderly patients with multiple health conditions?

+
Yes, physiotherapy is very safe when performed by trained professionals. We carefully assess all medical conditions, medications, and limitations before starting treatment. Exercises are gentle, age-appropriate, and customized to each patient's abilities. We coordinate with doctors and monitor vital signs during sessions for added safety.

How many sessions will my elderly parent need?

+
Duration varies by condition. Acute issues like post-surgery recovery may need 12-20 sessions. Chronic conditions like arthritis benefit from ongoing maintenance (2-3 sessions/week). Many seniors continue physiotherapy long-term to maintain mobility and prevent decline. We recommend starting with 8-12 sessions to assess progress.

What should we prepare for the first home visit?

+
Have all medical reports, medication lists, and doctor's prescriptions ready. Clear a comfortable space for exercises. Wear loose, comfortable clothing. Have a family member present for the first session. The therapist brings all necessary equipment. No special preparation is needed beyond this.

Do you accept insurance for senior citizen physiotherapy?

+
We provide detailed invoices and treatment documentation that can be submitted to insurance companies for reimbursement. Many health insurance policies cover physiotherapy. We recommend checking with your insurance provider about coverage. We also offer affordable packages to make treatment accessible for all seniors.

Can physiotherapy help with dementia or Alzheimer's patients?

+
Yes, physiotherapy helps dementia patients maintain physical function, reduce agitation, improve sleep, and enhance quality of life. We use simple exercises, familiar activities, and routine-based approaches. Physical activity can slow cognitive decline and reduce behavioral symptoms. Family involvement is crucial for success.
